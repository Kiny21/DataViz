Does music help with improving our mental health?

Music therapy, or MT, is the use of music to improve an individual's stress, mood, and overall mental health. MT is also recognized as an evidence-based practice, using music as a catalyst for "happy" hormones such as oxytocin.

The forthcoming pair of charts provides us with valuable insights into the extent of severity associated with mental health issues. The initial chart, the circular barchart, illuminates the connection between daily listening hours and the likelihood of experiencing conditions such as anxiety, depression, insomnia, or OCD. This chart portrays how these mental health concerns correspond to one's music consumption habits.

On the other hand, the second chart takes a different approach by categorizing participants based on the severity of their individual mental health disorders. It effectively condenses and presents data on how many survey participants have encountered varying degrees of severity within these mental illnesses.
These two visual representations collectively contribute to our understanding of the complex relationship between music consumption, mental health, and the distribution of these conditions among survey respondents.

The subsequent pair of scatterplots visually represents data regarding individuals' ages, their daily hours of listening to music, and additional factors such as their musical inclination, proficiency in foreign languages, and whether they engage in listening to music while working. These scatterplots provide insights into the correlations between age demographics and the varying durations spent on music consumption, shedding light on distinctions among individuals who are musicians or have an affinity for foreign languages, as well as those who incorporate music into their work routines.
